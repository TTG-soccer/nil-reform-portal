Policy overview

A proposed NIL and transfer portal reform framework for D1 men's college basketball.

Core principle

When a player transfers upward to a significantly better-resourced, historically stronger program, the gaining school owes a fee to the school losing the player. This protects smaller programs from being stripped of talent with no compensation and discourages blue-blood programs from using the portal as a free shopping cart.

Tier definitions

Fee formula

Fee = (X × tier_mult) + Y
X = minutes played this season × $75 per minute
Y = sum of all award bonuses (recent season = higher rate, prior seasons = lower rate)

Fee rules

In-conference transfers: always free — preserves rivalry competition.
Same-tier or downward transfers: always free.
No fee cap: fees scale freely with minutes and awards.
Fee recipient: 100% goes to the school losing the player.

Fee distribution

Of the total fee received, the losing school must reinvest at least 70% directly back into the basketball program.
